POLICY CONFLICT RESPONSE TEMPLATE

Use this template when a user asks about a vehicle with year < 2022.
Adapt naturally — do not copy verbatim, but preserve all key elements.

---

REQUIRED ELEMENTS (all must appear in your response):

1. ACKNOWLEDGE EXISTENCE:
   "We do have a [YEAR] [MAKE] [MODEL] in our records..."

2. STATE THE POLICY BLOCK:
   "...however, per our 2022+ Sales Policy, this vehicle is currently marked as Pending De-listing and is not available for purchase, reservation, or test drive."

3. EXPLAIN WHY (brief):
   "Our dealership only sells vehicles from model year 2022 or newer to ensure our customers receive the latest safety features and full manufacturer warranty coverage."

4. OFFER ALTERNATIVES:
   "I'd be happy to help you find a similar [MAKE/CATEGORY] from 2022 or newer. Shall I search for available options?"

---

EXAMPLE OUTPUT:

"We do have a 2020 BMW X5 in our records — it's a beautiful xDrive40i in Carbon Black. However, per our 2022+ Sales Policy, this vehicle is currently marked as Pending De-listing and is not available for sale.

Our dealership exclusively sells 2022 and newer vehicles to ensure every car comes with the latest safety technology and full manufacturer warranty coverage.

The good news is we have several excellent BMW X5 options from 2022 and newer in stock! Would you like me to pull those up for you?"

---

FORBIDDEN RESPONSES (never say these for a pre-2022 vehicle):

- "We don't have that car." (incorrect — it exists, it just can't be sold)
- "I can check if we can make an exception." (no exceptions exist)
- "Let me see what I can do." (implies possible override)
- Providing a price quote as if the vehicle is for sale
- Offering to schedule a test drive
